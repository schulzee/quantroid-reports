Verdict: HOLD / accumulate on cycle weakness Commercial Metals is the cheapest forward multiple in the US steel cohort (11.7x vs 14.4x median) and a high-quality-for-its-class EAF rebar producer riding North American metal margins at a three-year high, walled by 50% Section 232 tariffs and rebar anti-dumping duties. But the recovery is margin-led with volumes flat-to-down (rebar tons -4% YoY), the FY2025 trough EPS of $0.74 shows how violently spreads mean-revert, and ~$2.5B of debt-funded precast M&A near a cycle high lifted net debt to ~$3.1B. On a mid-cycle EPS near $4.00 the stock trades at roughly 19x — a full price for a commodity producer — and the analyst target of $78.45 sits only 3% above the quote. The setup rewards patience over chasing: own it, but accumulate on cycle weakness rather than at the spread peak.

Why (key reasons):

  • Cheapest forward valuation in the cohort — 11.71x P/E fwd and 10.9x EV/EBITDA vs cohort medians of 14.45x and 14.3x (cheapness rank #1 of NUE/STLD/CLF/CMC) — with the industry read flagging CMC as the best quality-adjusted long.
  • Policy-walled franchise riding a real up-leg: North America (~75% of sales) steel-products metal margin hit a three-year high of $623/ton (+31% YoY) on +20% pricing vs only +4% scrap, backstopped by 50% Section 232 tariffs and affirmative rebar AD/CVD holding imports near ~22%; downstream new-booking prices now exceed backlog, a forward margin tailwind.
  • Severe cyclicality with a full mid-cycle price — EPS ran $9.95 (2022 peak) to $0.74 (2025 trough) to $4.46 TTM; at $76 the market pays ~19x a ~$4.00 mid-cycle EPS and the analyst target ($78.45) is only +3.2% above the quote, so most of the recovery is already discounted.
  • Stepped-up balance-sheet and capital-allocation risk — ~$2.52B of debt-funded Foley/CP&P precast M&A executed near a cycle high, ~$600M FY2026 capex (4th micro mill), and ~$2.0B new senior notes lifted net debt to ~$3.1B (~2.9x mid-cycle EBITDA) from a prior near-net-cash posture.
  • Supply is the key down-leg risk — incoming US EAF capacity (including CMC's own micro mill starting June 2026) against US rebar demand guided +1-3% and flat shipments; management calls it 'manageable' and Q3 metal margin only 'very stable' as higher-cost scrap lags through, signaling the margin tailwind pauses near-term.
  • Tensar/Construction Solutions downstream pivot is a structural de-cyclicalization lever, not just M&A: the engineered-solutions and precast/fabrication build-out (Construction Solutions sales +98%, ~$2.5B Foley/CP&P) adds higher-margin, longer-cycle revenue (1-2yr fixed-price contracts, bookings now above backlog) that is the company's deliberate answer to raising its trough and reducing commodity cyclicality.
  • Low-carbon EAF/scrap-based production is a structural demand and relative-cost advantage: versus integrated blast-furnace rivals (notably cohort peer CLF), CMC's scrap-based steelmaking benefits from government Buy Clean / low-embodied-carbon procurement and Environmental Product Declarations, and would gain relative cost advantage if carbon is ever priced (EU CBAM already creating a carbon-border premium).

Scenarios & possible futures

At $76.05 the market is paying ~19x a ~$4.00 mid-cycle EPS (and ~11.7x the FY1 consensus near $6.63), implicitly treating near-peak North American metal margins as durable rather than cyclical; the +3.2% gap to the $78.45 analyst target says consensus already sees the spread-led recovery as largely captured. The single pivotal swing variable is the rebar-to-shredded-scrap spread (the metal margin, currently a three-year high at ~$623/ton) and the Section 232 / rebar-AD-CVD wall that backstops it -- a sustained ~10% expansion carries the stock toward the ~$96 up-cycle world, a sustained ~10% compression (or a tariff rollback that lifts import share back toward ~22%) drives it toward the ~$36 downcycle world. Everything between is the ~$56 mid-cycle base, where spreads normalize off the peak but the policy floor and secular data-center/IIJA demand hold.

Industry

Sector: the sector · stage: early-to-mid-upswing, recovering — the single biggest tell is synchronized metal-spread expansion (STLD +30%, CMC +31% YoY, NUE guiding Q1 expansion) on benign scrap while utilization (NUE 76%) and tonnage stay below mid-cycle, i.e., margin recovery leading a still-pending volume recovery · structural-change score 52/100 (0 = fully cyclical / eroding moats; 100 = structurally re-rated / entrenched).

The aggregate evidence places US steel in a price- and spread-led recovery off a clearly defined early-2025 earnings trough. Metal spreads are inflecting up sharply and synchronously while scrap input costs stay benign: STLD steel spread +30% YoY (+$86/ton sequentially, steel op income +73% q/q), CMC NA metal margin at a three-year high of $623/ton (+31% YoY), CLF ASP $1,048/ton on HRC +24% to ~$980, and NUE guiding Q1 2026 metal-margin expansion led by sheet. Supply risk is the key down-leg watch item — new EAF capacity is landing (NUE's ~3M-ton WV sheet mill 2027, CMC's 4th micro mill June 2026, STLD aluminum) — but it is partly offset by CLF's aggressive rationalization (idled Dearborn, closed Steelton/Conshohocken/Riverdale), 50% Section 232 tariffs plus rebar AD/CVD walling imports to ~22%, and lean channel inventories that set up restocking rather than a destocking overhang. Demand is constructive and broadening (data centers, grid/electrical, onshoring, infrastructure) but still volume-light — utilization remains below mid-cycle (NUE 76%, STLD 89%) and tonnage is flat-to-soft (CMC rebar -4%), meaning the recovery is being driven by margin first with volume upside still ahead. Net: a tariff-supported, spread-led upswing with widening margins on benign costs and depressed volumes, not a late-cycle peak.

Synthesis & verdict

Commercial Metals is a vertically integrated electric-arc-furnace producer of rebar and long products whose earnings are, in effect, a leveraged bet on North American steel metal margins — the North America Steel Group is ~75% of sales ($1.61B of $2.13B in Q2 FY2026). That segment is in a sharp, policy-driven up-leg: metal margin reached a three-year high of $623/ton (+31% YoY) as ~+20% pricing outran a +4% rise in scrap, lifting consolidated core EBITDA +114% YoY to $297.5M. The up-leg is genuinely supported by structure, not just spot noise — 50% Section 232 tariffs and affirmative anti-dumping/countervailing duties on imported rebar (Algeria 72.94%, Bulgaria, Egypt, Vietnam) wall off the classic import-driven down-leg, and the cohort sits in an early-to-mid upswing where margin recovery is leading a still-pending volume recovery on lean channel inventories.

The valuation is the crux. CMC screens as the cheapest name in its cohort on forward earnings (11.71x vs a 14.45x median) and EV/EBITDA (10.9x vs 14.3x), and the shared industry read tags it the best quality-adjusted long. But a cyclical commodity producer must be judged on mid-cycle, not trailing, earnings. The diluted-EPS record — -$1.81 (2010), $9.95 (2022 peak), $0.74 (2025 trough), $4.46 TTM — shows both the violence of the cycle and a structurally higher floor from the EAF cost-curve shift. A mid-cycle EPS near $4.00, biased modestly above the simple average for the tariff/EAF re-rating, is the right anchor. At a mid-cycle multiple of ~14x that is roughly $56 of value; at the $76.05 price the market is paying ~19x mid-cycle EPS, a full price that already capitalizes much of the recovery. The reverse read on the analyst target reinforces this: $78.45 is only +3.2% above the quote, the thinnest positive upside in the cohort.

Quality and capital allocation are above the cohort average but the bar just rose. CMC has grown its dividend every year since the GFC (an 11% hike in Q2 FY2026) and accelerated buybacks to ~$199M in FY2025, shrinking the share count from 122.4M (2022) to 110.9M. Against that, management pivoted aggressively into M&A at the top of the cycle — ~$2.52B of cash for the Foley and CP&P precast platforms, funded by ~$2.0B of new senior notes (5.750% 2033s, 6.000% 2035s) — and is spending ~$600M of FY2026 capex, ~$300M on a fourth micro mill in West Virginia. Net debt of ~$3.1B (~2.9x mid-cycle EBITDA) replaces a prior near-net-cash posture; the diversification into Construction Solutions (sales +98%) and downstream backlog (highest bookings since late FY2022, led by data centers and energy) is strategically sound, but buying capacity and footprint into peak pricing carries cycle-timing risk that only seasons favorably if demand absorbs the new supply.

Net: a good business at a fair-to-full price near a spread high. The risk is not balance-sheet distress — CMC is far sturdier than cohort distress name CLF — but paying a mid-cycle-rich multiple for peak-ish margins just as new EAF capacity lands and Q3 margins are guided only 'stable.' The reward case is that tariffs plus secular construction demand (onshoring, grid, data centers) hold spreads elevated for longer and the WV micro mill and precast synergies push FY2027 earnings above the $7.12 consensus. That asymmetry argues for owning the name through the cycle but sourcing the position on weakness, not at the peak.

Key risks & watch items

  • Rebar demand is overwhelmingly non-residential & public-infrastructure, and rate-sensitive — CMC's rebar/long-product volume is tied to highways, bridges, commercial/institutional structures and public works rather than housing, so the demand path hinges on nonresidential construction starts (Dodge Momentum Index, currently +14.1% YoY but propped almost entirely by data centers) against a still-contractionary Architecture Billings Index (48.3), leaving traditional/broad nonresidential demand flat-to-soft and exposed to higher-for-longer interest rates that delay commercial groundbreaks 12-18 months out.
  • IIJA infrastructure-spend timing and reauthorization risk — The durable, rate-independent leg of rebar demand is federal Infrastructure Investment & Jobs Act (IIJA) highway/bridge funding, whose outlays are back-end-loaded through roughly 2026-2028; the timing of dollars reaching steel-intensive groundbreaks is lumpy and slower than appropriations, and any clawback, reprioritization, or failure to reauthorize the surface-transportation program after IIJA runs off would remove a key multi-year volume floor for CMC.
  • Metal-margin / scrap-spread mean reversion from a three-year high — Earnings are a leveraged bet on the North America steel-products metal margin ($623/ton, a 3-yr high, +31% YoY), and the FY2025 trough EPS of $0.74 versus the $9.95 FY2022 peak shows how violently the scrap-to-selling-price spread reverts; management already guides Q3 FY2026 margin only 'very stable' as higher-cost scrap lags through, signaling the tailwind pauses near-term.
  • New EAF long-product supply landing into flat rebar demand — CMC's own 4th micro mill (West Virginia, June 2026 start) plus competing new domestic EAF/merchant-bar and rebar capacity is being added against US rebar demand guided only +1-3% with shipments flat (CMC rebar tons -4% YoY); if net new industry tons outpace the modest demand growth, the spread recovery the price already capitalizes recompresses, the central down-leg risk management calls 'manageable.'
  • Section 232 tariff and rebar AD/CVD durability is policy-dependent — The bull case rests on the 50% Section 232 tariff (restructured April 2026 to full customs value) and affirmative rebar anti-dumping/countervailing duties holding spreads elevated; because Section 232 is executive-discretion tariff policy subject to administration change, bilateral carve-outs/quotas (UK already at 25%), and legal challenge to presidential tariff authority, a rollback is a real two-sided catalyst the elevated-spread thesis is exposed to.
  • Electricity, graphite-electrode and power-availability exposure of the EAF model — EAF economics are scrap plus power, yet the metal-margin framing centers on scrap alone; rising industrial electricity prices, graphite-electrode and alloy costs, and grid constraints (ironically intensified by the very data-center load cited as a demand tailwind) are direct conversion-margin risks and a siting/operating risk for the new West Virginia micro mill.
  • Stepped-up leverage from cycle-top M&A — Management deployed ~$2.52B of debt-funded cash into Foley/CP&P precast acquisitions with metal margin at a 3-yr high, funded by ~$2.0B new senior notes (5.750% 2033s, 6.000% 2035s), lifting net debt to ~$3.1B (~2.9x mid-cycle EBITDA) from a prior near-net-cash posture; concurrent ~$600M FY2026 capex during a margin-normalization window could strain FCF and leverage if a downcycle coincides with peak spending.
  • China oversupply / global scrap and import pressure on the tariff wall — Record Chinese steel exports amid weak Chinese construction depress world steel and global scrap prices, which is precisely what makes the US tariff wall load-bearing; persistent diverted tonnage seeking ways around trade barriers and softer global scrap both pressure CMC's relative price premium and its export-adjacent Poland segment.
  • Poland/Europe segment carries distinct CBAM, energy and demand risks — Though small (~$200M/qtr), the Europe segment faces weak EU construction, high European power costs, and EU ETS/CBAM carbon costs that diverge from North America and have historically swung CMC results, making it more than the FX footnote it is currently treated as.

  • Dodge Momentum Index rolls off its ~264 April-2026 high and falls through ~220 on a 3-month trend, AND the gain narrows so even the data-center leg cools (headline YoY decelerating toward the ~+5.8% ex-data-center pace) -- the planning pipeline that leads CMC's nonres rebar orders ~12 months is contracting.
  • Architecture Billings Index deteriorates further below its 48.3 reading toward 45 and stays there for 6+ months -- confirming broad private nonresidential demand is rolling over before IIJA/data-center volume backfills it, the verdict's core demand risk.
  • Rebar-to-shredded-scrap spread compresses materially off the $623/ton 3-yr high -- independent CRU/Platts rebar prices softening while shredded/BUS scrap firms -- compressing the EAF metal margin that drives CMC profitability regardless of volumes (the FY2025 $0.74 trough EPS shows the violence of this reversion).
