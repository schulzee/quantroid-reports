Industry

Sector: the sector · stage: early-to-mid-upswing, recovering — the single biggest tell is synchronized metal-spread expansion (STLD +30%, CMC +31% YoY, NUE guiding Q1 expansion) on benign scrap while utilization (NUE 76%) and tonnage stay below mid-cycle, i.e., margin recovery leading a still-pending volume recovery · structural-change score 52/100 (0 = fully cyclical / eroding moats; 100 = structurally re-rated / entrenched).

The aggregate evidence places US steel in a price- and spread-led recovery off a clearly defined early-2025 earnings trough. Metal spreads are inflecting up sharply and synchronously while scrap input costs stay benign: STLD steel spread +30% YoY (+$86/ton sequentially, steel op income +73% q/q), CMC NA metal margin at a three-year high of $623/ton (+31% YoY), CLF ASP $1,048/ton on HRC +24% to ~$980, and NUE guiding Q1 2026 metal-margin expansion led by sheet. Supply risk is the key down-leg watch item — new EAF capacity is landing (NUE's ~3M-ton WV sheet mill 2027, CMC's 4th micro mill June 2026, STLD aluminum) — but it is partly offset by CLF's aggressive rationalization (idled Dearborn, closed Steelton/Conshohocken/Riverdale), 50% Section 232 tariffs plus rebar AD/CVD walling imports to ~22%, and lean channel inventories that set up restocking rather than a destocking overhang. Demand is constructive and broadening (data centers, grid/electrical, onshoring, infrastructure) but still volume-light — utilization remains below mid-cycle (NUE 76%, STLD 89%) and tonnage is flat-to-soft (CMC rebar -4%), meaning the recovery is being driven by margin first with volume upside still ahead. Net: a tariff-supported, spread-led upswing with widening margins on benign costs and depressed volumes, not a late-cycle peak.

Synthesis & verdict

Cleveland-Cliffs is the deep-cyclical, balance-sheet-levered name in US steel - the structural opposite of the low-cost EAF compounders. It is an integrated blast-furnace/BOF producer built through debt-funded acquisition (AK Steel and ArcelorMittal USA in 2020, Stelco in 2024), concentrated in North American flat-rolled and automotive steel (~33% of shipments). That integrated footprint sits higher on the cost curve than mini-mill peers, so CLF loses money at the trough (operating income of -$34M in 2024, EBIT of $120M and net income of -$550M on a TTM basis) where low-cost EAF operators stay profitable. There is no durable cost moat; the franchise rests on concentrated domestic auto supply and Section 232 tariff protection, both of which help pricing but neither of which is a price-setting advantage.

The balance sheet is the central risk. Net debt of ~$6.9B against trough TTM EBITDA of ~$600M is roughly 11.6x - by far the highest in the cohort versus under 1x for Nucor and Steel Dynamics - and gross debt climbed to $7.1B in 2024 (from $3.7B in 2023) to fund the Stelco deal, while cash sits at just $60M. Free cash flow is negative (-$250M TTM, -$546M in 2024), and capital allocation has been aggressive: share count nearly doubled from ~294M (2019) to ~497M, buybacks were procyclical (shares fell after the 2021 peak), and the common dividend is negligible. Maturities are termed-out into 2027-2030 and refinanced opportunistically, so near-term liquidity is manageable, but the equity is a thin, levered claim that compresses quickly if the cycle stalls.

The setup is a high-torque play on the steel cycle. US HRC bottomed in late-2025 around $650-720/ton, sits near $760 now, and 2026 consensus assumes a gradual recovery toward $850 on restocking, onshoring and infrastructure demand, capacity discipline, and reaffirmed/expanded Section 232 tariffs. CLF has the most operating leverage in the group - roughly $1.4-1.6B of EBITDA swing per $100/ton of HRC on ~16Mt of shipments - so it gains the most on the way up and loses the most at the bottom. Layered on top is idiosyncratic automotive risk: Detroit production cuts, the EV transition, and UAW labor cycles hit CLF volumes and fixed-cost absorption harder than they hit diversified peers. Return outcomes are dominated by where HRC and auto build rates go, and by entry timing relative to the trough.

Valuation is cheap on normalized numbers but for a reason. At ~4.6x mid-cycle EV/EBITDA on ~$2.4B of mid-cycle EBITDA, CLF screens as the cheapest name in the cohort - against ~18.3x on depressed TTM EBITDA and 11.6x net leverage. Mid-cycle fair value sits around $10.50 (range $4-22), anchored on ~5x mid-cycle EV/EBITDA and mid-cycle EPS power near $1.30, but that value is only realized if HRC normalizes; the wide range reflects the leverage. At $8.10 - below the $9.50 mean analyst target, inside a $6-14 spread, with a HOLD rating and a 'down' EPS-revision trend - the market is pricing closer to a sustained trough than to a clean mid-cycle recovery. The disciplined approach is to treat CLF as a tactical, trough-anchored call option, buying near the 52-week low where the asymmetry is real, rather than paying up into a recovery that is not yet washed out.

Falsifiers & monitoring

What would prove the thesis wrong:

  • US HRC spot relapses and holds below ~$700/mt (vs ~$1,190 now): the metal spread that drives CLF's ~$1.4-1.6B EBITDA-per-$100/ton operating leverage collapses, returning the integrated/BOF cost base to losses and mapping to the $3.50 downcycle.
  • Section 232 is cut from 50% or broadened with bilateral carve-outs (UK already at 25%), and finished-steel import share re-expands from ~15% back toward the >22% Q1-2025 level, pushing AISI capacity utilization back down toward ~76% (the prior-year YTD rate) and erasing the domestic price premium.
  • Dodge Momentum Index rolls over below ~240 (the data-center planning pipeline cresting) while the ABI stays in contraction below ~48 (currently 48.3, sub-50 since Jan 2023): the only growth leg of nonresidential demand fades with no broad-based offset.
  • Light-vehicle SAAR breaks below ~15.5M (vs ~15.9M April, already -7.1% YoY for an eighth straight month), hitting CLF's ~33% auto book and fixed-cost absorption harder than diversified EAF peers.
  • Hyperscaler 2026 capex guides are cut from the ~$635-700B run-rate (or data-center construction stalls below ~$47B annualized) at the same time manufacturing-construction spend keeps falling (~-15% YoY, -44% in fab construction), removing the onshoring/data-center demand layer that offsets the rolling-over CHIPS wave.
  • Incoming new EAF capacity (Nucor's ~3M-ton WV sheet mill, CMC's 4th micro mill) ramps into flat-to-soft end demand and visibly recompresses HRC spreads even with tariffs intact, signaling oversupply rather than a tightening cycle.

Highest-signal things to watch:

  • US HRC spot price (SteelBenchmarker / CRU / Trading Economics) relative to the ~$700 / ~$850 / ~$900-1,000 scenario bands, read against the current ~$1,190/mt (weekly) — HRC is the single best exit/entry trigger: it directly sets CLF's ~$1.4-1.6B EBITDA swing per $100/ton on ~16Mt shipments and is the variable that places the stock on the $3.50/$10.50/$22 ladder. A sustained break below ~$700 is the exit; a hold above ~$900 is the up-cycle confirmation.
  • Section 232 rate/scope plus finished-steel import share (Commerce SIMA license data) and AISI weekly capacity-utilization rate (monthly) — This is the clearest swing factor for the domestic price floor: import share at ~15% and utilization ~82% underpin the current spread. Any tariff rollback or carve-out, import share re-expanding toward ~22%, or utilization falling toward ~76% would flip the floor independently of any company guidance.
  • Dodge Momentum Index (DMI) cross-checked against the AIA/Deltek Architecture Billings Index (ABI) (monthly) — DMI leads nonresidential steel orders ~12 months and ABI ~9-12 months; they currently disagree (DMI 264.2 / +14.1% YoY vs ABI 48.3 in contraction). DMI breaking above ~270 with broad-based gains would corroborate the up-cycle, while DMI rolling under ~240 with ABI still sub-48 signals the data-center-only pipeline cresting toward the downcycle.
  • Hyperscaler 2026 capex guides (Microsoft/Meta/Alphabet/Amazon) plus Census data-center and total manufacturing-construction spending run-rate (quarterly) — The data-center/AI capex (~$635-700B, rising) is the differentiated demand layer offsetting the -15% YoY fade in manufacturing/fab construction; a cut to these guides or a stall in data-center construction would remove the structural support the bull case leans on.
  • Light-vehicle sales SAAR (NADA/Cox/FRED TOTALSA) and North American production schedules (S&P Global Mobility) (monthly) — CLF's ~33% auto exposure makes the build rate an idiosyncratic volume driver; SAAR is ~15.9M and -7.1% YoY for eight straight months, so a break below ~15.5M caps sheet-volume upside even if spreads widen, whereas a recovery above ~16.5M supports the up-cycle case.
