Industry

Sector: the sector · stage: early-to-mid-upswing, recovering — the single biggest tell is synchronized metal-spread expansion (STLD +30%, CMC +31% YoY, NUE guiding Q1 expansion) on benign scrap while utilization (NUE 76%) and tonnage stay below mid-cycle, i.e., margin recovery leading a still-pending volume recovery · structural-change score 52/100 (0 = fully cyclical / eroding moats; 100 = structurally re-rated / entrenched).

The aggregate evidence places US steel in a price- and spread-led recovery off a clearly defined early-2025 earnings trough. Metal spreads are inflecting up sharply and synchronously while scrap input costs stay benign: STLD steel spread +30% YoY (+$86/ton sequentially, steel op income +73% q/q), CMC NA metal margin at a three-year high of $623/ton (+31% YoY), CLF ASP $1,048/ton on HRC +24% to ~$980, and NUE guiding Q1 2026 metal-margin expansion led by sheet. Supply risk is the key down-leg watch item — new EAF capacity is landing (NUE's ~3M-ton WV sheet mill 2027, CMC's 4th micro mill June 2026, STLD aluminum) — but it is partly offset by CLF's aggressive rationalization (idled Dearborn, closed Steelton/Conshohocken/Riverdale), 50% Section 232 tariffs plus rebar AD/CVD walling imports to ~22%, and lean channel inventories that set up restocking rather than a destocking overhang. Demand is constructive and broadening (data centers, grid/electrical, onshoring, infrastructure) but still volume-light — utilization remains below mid-cycle (NUE 76%, STLD 89%) and tonnage is flat-to-soft (CMC rebar -4%), meaning the recovery is being driven by margin first with volume upside still ahead. Net: a tariff-supported, spread-led upswing with widening margins on benign costs and depressed volumes, not a late-cycle peak.

Structural shifts in play:

  • AI data-center & grid build-out (+ onshoring) creating a durable, steel-intensive new demand layer that is replacing the rolling-over CHIPS-fab wave (direction: tailwind; horizon: 3-10y; impact: high) — Independently visible: DMI April 2026 +14.1% YoY with data centers the single largest driver (commercial ex-data-centers only +5.8% YoY); data-center construction ~$47B annualized (~5x in two years) and power-infrastructure spend guided to rebound to $27.8B in 2026 from $16.5B in 2025; hyperscaler 2026 capex ~$635-700B and rising. A hyperscale facility consumes 12,000-20,000 tons of steel, and NUE can supply ~95% of a data center's steel. This offsets the -15% YoY / -44%-from-peak fade in semiconductor-fab construction -- a mix shift toward AI/grid demand.
  • Persistent / intensified trade protection (Section 232 restructured to 50% on full customs value, derivatives expanded) holding domestic utilization and a US price premium far above world prices (direction: tailwind; horizon: 1-3y; impact: high) — April 6, 2026 proclamation applies the 50% rate to the FULL customs value (not just metal content) and closes the derivative-undervaluation loophole; finished-steel import share fell from >22% (Q1 2025) to ~15% (YTD 2026) with imports down ~46% YoY, lifting capacity utilization toward ~82% and supporting a ~42% YoY rise in US HRC -- even as world prices sit near cycle lows. The main risk is policy reversal or bilateral carve-outs (UK already at 25%).
  • EAF / scrap-based (mini-mill) capacity gains over integrated blast-furnace steelmaking -- now ~67-72% of US output -- lower cost curve, lower carbon, operational flexibility (direction: tailwind for EAF players (NUE/STLD/CMC); headwind/risk for integrated exposure (CLF). Double-edged near-term: incoming new EAF capacity (NUE ~3M-ton WV sheet mill 2027, CMC 4th micro mill June 2026, STLD aluminum) is the top supply risk to spreads.; horizon: 3-10y; impact: high) — EAF is now ~67-72% of US crude steel (vs. ~29% globally), so the US fleet is structurally lower-cost/lower-carbon. Cohort evidence: the one name in losses is the integrated, ore-based producer (CLF, TTM net loss), while flexible low-fixed-cost EAF mills (NUE/STLD/CMC) print expanding metal spreads on benign scrap -- the cost curve now punishes the legacy integrated model; management flags incoming EAF supply as the chief down-leg risk.
  • Green-steel / decarbonization transition has STALLED in the US under current federal policy -- hydrogen-DRI projects shelved, legacy coal assets extended (direction: mixed -- modest medium-term headwind to the 'green-steel re-rating' thesis; near-term neutral-to-tailwind for low-carbon EAF on relative carbon advantage (e.g. EU CBAM), but the US clean-steel capex catalyst has weakened; horizon: 3-10y; impact: medium) — Cleveland-Cliffs DROPPED its planned ~2.5M-ton Ohio hydrogen-DRI/EAF project and shifted back toward extending coal-based assets after the 2026 Paris exit and DOE cancellation of >$2B in hydrogen-hub funding; analysts note EAF/green-DRI gains are 'overshadowed by blast-furnace expansion, coal-based DRI growth, and reinvestment in legacy coal assets.' So the secular decarbonization tailwind that could have re-rated low-carbon producers has materially slowed in the US, while EU CBAM keeps a carbon-border premium alive for exporters.

The question that decides the thesis: Does the data-center/AI-led demand layer (driving DMI +14% YoY and hyperscaler capex toward ~$700B) plus the restructured 50%-full-value Section 232 floor (imports cut from ~22% to ~15%, utilization rising toward ~82%, HRC +~42% YoY) sustain the US metal-spread and price premium through 2026-2027 -- BEFORE the wave of incoming new EAF capacity (NUE WV, CMC micro mill, plus a falling auto SAAR, a still-contractionary ABI, the rolling-over CHIPS-fab construction cycle, and any tariff rollback) outpaces the secular demand and recompresses spreads?

The catch is that the market already prices this quality, leaving thinner relative upside than CMC's cheaper forward multiple or CLF's levered torque — NUE is the premium-quality core holding you pay up for, not the cohort's value entry. The call shifts to clearly-cheap if sheet/HRC spreads accelerate well beyond the modest +1% ASP base so FY26/FY27 EPS ($9.78/$13.41) prove conservative, or if NUE de-rates toward CMC's ~12x forward; it weakens if the volume recovery stalls with utilization stuck near 76%, or if 2026-2027 EAF supply (including NUE's own WV mill) pressures spreads before demand absorbs it.

Headline pair: LONG CMC / SHORT STLD — Both are low-cost EAF producers riding the same long/flat-product spread recovery, but CMC trades at ~11.7x forward / ~10.9x EV/EBITDA versus STLD at ~15.5x / ~17.8x — a wide multiple gap for broadly similar mini-mill exposure and only a one-notch quality difference. Long the cheaper name, short the richer. Catalyst: CMC downstream margin inflection (bookings now above backlog price) and rebar AD/CVD flowing into earnings, narrowing the relative-multiple gap versus STLD's already-priced execution premium. · Risk: STLD's aluminum ramp (~$1.4B through-cycle EBITDA target) surprises positively or its superior returns (ROIC 10.9%, ROE 15.3%) and execution keep sustaining the premium, so the spread widens against the pair.

Synthesis & verdict

Earnings are commodity-cyclical (NI $0.7B -> $7.6B -> $1.7B over 2020-25), so valuation rests on through-cycle figures. Mid-cycle EPS is ~$11-13 (the 10-yr average, itself lifted by the 2021-22 steel supercycle). At $249 that is ~18-23x mid-cycle — expensive for a cyclical, which historically trades ~10-15x mid-cycle.

A through-cycle DCF (NOPAT ~$2.9B, 9.5% WACC, ~4% mature growth) centers ~$184; through-cycle steel multiples (~7x EV/EBITA) imply ~$135. Both sit below the $249 price.

Consensus targets ~$244 (~ price) and models FY26/27 EPS of $14.68/$15.94 — capitalizing the recovery year. A cycle-normalized view caps lower; the disagreement is recovery-extrapolation versus cycle-normalization.

Multi-year: a quality cyclical worth owning, but at a premium to mid-cycle value and at the 52-wk high it offers no margin of safety — the disciplined entry is the next downcycle (roughly $150-180). Near-term: no setup, and not a short given the low-cost position and onshoring demand.

Key risks & watch items

  • Section 232 tariff dependency (policy-reversible) — Nucor's mid-cycle margin assumptions ride on the Section 232 steel tariff that was doubled from 25% to 50% effective ~June 4, 2025, which holds US hot-rolled coil at a wide premium to world prices; this premium is administratively set and can be cut, granted product exclusions, or reversed by a future administration or trade deal (e.g., differentiated UK treatment), making it the single largest unmodeled swing factor on the price deck.
  • Import surge caps the price cycle — Even with Section 232 in place, sustained high US prices historically pull in imports within ~2-3 quarters via tariff-exempt/uncovered countries, product exclusions, transshipment (e.g., routing through Mexico or Southeast Asia), and substitution into finished/downstream goods, the mechanism that has ended every prior US steel price spike and that the thesis's durable-pricing assumption understates.
  • Back-loaded growth-capex execution risk — Nucor is funding a concentrated, multi-year growth program (the ~$3.1B Apple Grove, West Virginia sheet mill plus DRI, galvanizing lines and micromills, with cumulative growth spend cited around $10-14B); greenfield sheet mills have a track record of startup delays and multi-quarter ramps, and depreciation plus pre-run-rate operating drag can weigh on earnings before these assets contribute, while elevated capex pressures free cash flow if the steel cycle softens during the build.
  • Late-cycle downstream M&A at premium multiples — Nucor's diversification into less-cyclical adjacencies (CHI Overhead Doors ~$3B in 2022, Rytec and Southwest Data Products in 2024, Volkert engineering, towers/structures, insulated metal panels) was partly executed near peak earnings/valuations, and the Steel Products segment housing CHI has already seen earnings fall from 2022 peaks on residential softness, raising integration and capital-allocation risk that high multiples paid late in the cycle destroy value if end-markets turn.

Falsifiers & monitoring

What would prove the thesis wrong:

  • HRC-busheling scrap metal spread compresses below ~$330 (vs ~$410 now) and stays there for two-plus months, signalling the EAF margin engine has fallen below the ~$400 mid-cycle assumption toward the bear-case ~$300.
  • Dodge Momentum Index falls below 180 (from 205) for a sustained stretch, pointing to a nonres-construction rollover ~12 months out across the ~60% of demand that drives the base case.
  • Section 232 tariff is cut from 50% back toward 25%, or broad country/product exemptions are granted, removing the domestic price floor; confirmed by finished-steel import penetration re-accelerating above ~25% (from ~21%).
  • Census real manufacturing-construction spending breaks decisively below ~$210B (from ~$235B) as the 2022-24 CHIPS/IRA build wave completes without replacement starts, draining the fastest-growing (plate/bar/structural) demand pool.
  • US light-vehicle SAAR drops below ~15M on consumer-credit stress, pressuring the high-margin auto sheet/SBQ pool (~15% of demand) and removing the steady-demand ballast in the base case.
  • HRC spot price falls below ~$720/ton (from ~$840) for a sustained period, undercutting the ~$800 level needed to support mid-cycle+ EPS regardless of where the headline tariff sits.

Highest-signal things to watch:

  • HRC-busheling scrap metal spread (CRU/Platts HRC spot minus busheling scrap, ~$410 now) (weekly) — Single best exit/entry trigger: spread, not price, drives EAF mini-mill profitability. Holding above ~$400 confirms the $150 base case; sustained break below ~$330 flags the slide toward the $105 bear case. The most direct, real-time read on Nucor's earnings power.
  • Dodge Momentum Index (205, rising) (monthly) — Leads nonres construction starts (~60% of demand) by ~12 months. Above 230 underwrites the $200 bull; the 195-215 band supports the $150 base; below 180 is the leading tell for the $105 bear well before it shows in orders.
  • Section 232 tariff rate (50%) and finished-steel import penetration share (~21%) (event) — Sets the domestic price floor that cushions the downside in the HOLD thesis. Any rollback toward 25% or broad exemptions, or import share climbing back above ~25%, is a step-change risk that re-rates the whole scenario ladder downward.
  • Census real manufacturing-construction spending (~$235B annualized, flat-to-down) (monthly) — Tracks the fastest-growing reshoring demand pool. Re-acceleration above the ~$240B peak supports the bull; a break below ~$210B signals the CHIPS/IRA wave is completing without replacement, draining plate/bar/structural demand toward the bear.
  • US light-vehicle SAAR (~16.2M, flat) (monthly) — Proxy for the high-margin auto sheet/SBQ pool (~15% of demand) and the steadier counterweight to construction cyclicality. A drop below ~15M removes base-case ballast and signals consumer-credit-driven demand erosion.
  • IIJA / federal highway and transmission outlays (DOT), ~55-60% of the $550B incremental still to spend through 2028 (quarterly) — Long-duration, policy-locked rebar/plate/structural demand. The pace of outlay conversion to mill orders is the durable tailwind underpinning the base case and a contributor to the bull's infrastructure-peak leg.
